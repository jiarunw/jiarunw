The SeasonDepth

In the application of outdoor mobile robotics and autonomous driving, it is universally recognized that the long-term robustness and stability of learning-based depth prediction under various environments has been the bottleneck of lifelong trustworthy autonomy \cite{badino2011visual,Sattler2018CVPR}. Although monocular depth prediction has been well studied recently in the research community, however, there is little work on robust depth prediction across different environments, e.g. changing illumination and seasons, which is owing to the lack of a diverse real-world dataset containing various scenarios and the corresponding benchmark.

The Challenge

In order to raise attention among the ICRA community to solve the challenging lifelong learning-based depth prediction problem, we plan to organize the SeasonDepth Prediction Challenge based on our new monocular depth prediction dataset, SeasonDepth \cite{SeasonDepth} {\color{blue} \href{https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.04408}{[link]}}, which contains multi-traverse outdoor images from changing environments. It is also the first depth prediction dataset with diverse road scenes to benchmark the depth prediction performance under different environmental conditions. To the best of our knowledge, we are the first to quantitatively evaluate the accuracy and robustness of monocular depth prediction across dramatically changing environments, setting up two tracks for both supervised learning and self-supervised learning-based methods. We believe our competition will contribute to flourish the long-term robust perception research among the ICRA community with our dataset and benchmark.
